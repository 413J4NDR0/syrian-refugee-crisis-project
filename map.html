Information and Sources

This map provides three detailed views of the Syrian Refugee Crisis in the Mediterranean and the United States:

  • The concentration of Syrian refugees and asylum seekers in 2015 and 2016;
  • The total distribution of photographs in the Associated Press Database from June 2015 to May 2017;
  • The distribution of Associated Press photographs published by the eight news agencies of interest from June 2015 to May 2017

Change the current view using the Change View menu.
Switch between the United States and the Mediterranean using the button labeled USA or Mediterranean, respectively.

The intensity of the color provides a general sense of the information in question for each country, while hovering over a particular country provides the exact information. Click on a country to view a gallery of the most cited Associated Press images in news articles. The gallery can be navigated using the left and right arrow keys.

Data for Syrian refugees and asylum seekers in the Mediterranean was taken from the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), while refugee data in the United States was from US News & World Report. According to the UNHCR,

  • Refugees are individuals recognised under the 1951 Convention relating to the Status of Refugees and subsequent revisions, and since 2007 refugee-like situations;
  • Asylum-seekers are individuals who have sought international protection and whose claims for refugee status have not yet been determined

Important Note: The UNHCR has the category Various/Unknown used for individuals who have multiple nationalities or whose nationality could not be ascertained; countries with large refugee population tend to have significant fluctuations in this category on a year-to-year basis. In particular, Greece had 22,160 individuals with this classification in 2015 and 12,833 in 2016. Although a significant portion of these refugees could certainly be of Syrian origin, the map only reflects the Syrian refugee populations the UNHCR was able to verify.
